Disussion

This page discusses the proposed method and attempts to put findings back into biological context.

Activity

Definition of activity

Activity was defined by an arbitrary threshold. This would make sense to provide a more rational definition of activity, possibly relying on morphological measurements as well as speed. For instance, the centre of mass of an animal can remain unchanged despite the animal moving (e.g. turning, slight tentacle motions).

Temporal structure

The presented binomial generalised mixed effect model does not capture the structural pattern of activity. For instance, activity seems to happen in bursts in most animals. This is problematic, because it involves that activity at a given time $t_m$ depends on a previous state $t_{n-1}$. In this respect, it would be interesting to model activity using a generative model such as two states Markov chain.

Day/Night state is not independent from time

Another weakness of the proposed model (but also the experiment) is that night and day status are correlated with time since there is a 24h period. Therefore, it is difficult to take apart the effect of both variable. Preliminary experiments in the laboratory have tried to alter the period, but results have not been analysed yet. In addition, both variable could be combined into an explicitely periodic variable.

Tracking

Lack of ground truth

For this project, no ground truth data was available. Having a collection of annotated images of snails would have been a very valuable resource. Without ground truth data, it is very hard to trust the robustness and accuracy of the proposed method or to improve it. For instance, the difference observed between day and night could, in principle, come from the fact that the tracking tool detect motion betted with the night frames. It was nevertheless promising to see that the measurements of speed were biologically sound. In addition, preliminary visual confirmation could be performed by plotting inferred position on the image. Measurement of other features such as area appeared very noisy. This could be because snails are highly deformable. It may also be explained by the lack of robustness to find contours in the tracking pipeline. For this reason, only speed was used to infer activity.

Back to the lab

The findings of this preliminary analysis will gives valuable clues to the experimentalists. The most critical point is the very strong decrease in activity over time. It probably arises from the fact that snails were not fed during the experiment, or that the environment was very stable (no changes in humidity or temperature). It will be essential to prevent this incidentally induced inactivity before investigating sleep in land snails any further. It nevertheless is very encouraging to observe such a clear differential of activity between day and night. In addition, it seems that young snails are more active and that Helix asperta is the most active species.

Investigation of sleep in this new model remains at a very early stage. This research was done in close collaboration with experimentalists and the provided tools (tracking and modelling) could be used to analyse future experiments with minor modifications once experimental issues have been addressed.
